I have dedicated my entire career to precision medicine—spanning genetics, tissue engineering, and pharmaceutical precision drug development. Now, my work is focused on causal AI, bridging machine learning, biology, and healthcare to create intelligent, proactive, and truly patient-centered medicine.

Drugs are designed for populations, not individuals. Interventions are tested in clinical trials that tell us what works on average, not what works for you. Patients bounce between specialists, prescriptions, and treatments, yet we still fail to answer the most important question:

Why did this disease develop in the first place?

I’m building the first causal map of health—an open-source AI model that charts disease progression, interventions, and prevention strategies. Unlike black-box machine learning models, causal AI doesn’t just predict—it explains. It uncovers the root causes of disease, allowing us to identify which treatments work, for whom, and why. This isn’t another predictive model; it’s a new framework for understanding and controlling health.

We need systems that actually understand causality.
